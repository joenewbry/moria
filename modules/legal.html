Implementation Roadmap
Four Phases to Full Capability
Building the legal assistant incrementally, starting with document understanding and progressing to proactive deadline management.
PHASE 1

Document Upload & Classification

Vision LLM processes scanned and digital documents, classifying them by type and extracting key information.

  • Vision LLM document ingestion
  • Automatic document type classification
  • Key entity extraction (dates, parties, amounts)
  • Organized document storage with metadata
PHASE 2

Legal Knowledge Base

Connect to authoritative legislation sources and build a searchable local knowledge base from user documents.

  • UK Lex API integration via MCP server
  • Legislation search and retrieval
  • ChromaDB embeddings for uploaded documents
  • Markdown skill files for common legal tasks
PHASE 3

Multi-Model Review

Draft legal documents with multi-pass validation to catch hallucinations and ensure safety constraints.

  • Local LLM generates draft letters and responses
  • Validation pass checks for fabricated citations
  • No case law generation (safety constraint)
  • Human review flagging for anything requiring legal citation
PHASE 4

Deadline Tracking & Alerts

Proactive monitoring of legal deadlines with calendar integration and automatic reminders.

  • Extract deadlines from legal documents
  • Calendar integration for timeline visualization
  • Proactive voice and notification alerts
  • Limitation period awareness and warnings

Design Philosophy
Key Decisions
Safety-first design choices informed by real-world legal AI failures and the hard-won lessons of UK legal technology companies.

No Case Law Generation

Following Garfield AI's safety constraint: CUBE will never generate or propose case law citations. Anything requiring legal citation is flagged for human review. This eliminates the most dangerous class of legal AI hallucination â€” fabricated cases that look authoritative but don't exist.

"Panel of Judges" Validation

Inspired by Luminance's multi-model consensus approach. Every draft goes through a validation pass that specifically checks for hallucinated citations, fabricated statute numbers, and invented legal concepts. Multiple models must agree before output is presented.

Legislation Search, Not Advice

CUBE searches and explains legislation using the authoritative UK Lex API, but never gives legal advice. It can tell you what the Consumer Rights Act 2015 says about faulty goods, but it won't tell you whether you have a case. That distinction is critical and maintained throughout the system.

Human-in-the-Loop

All drafted documents require explicit human review and approval before use. CUBE presents drafts with highlighted areas that need human judgment, explains its reasoning in plain language, and never sends anything on the user's behalf without confirmation.
